Tuna Mayo Onigiri

A Classic Japanese Rice Ball Filled With Creamy Tuna!

Ingredients (Makes 4-6 onigiri)
  • 1 can (5 oz / ~140g) tuna in water or oil, drained
  • 2-3 tbsp Japanese mayonnaise (e.g., Kewpie; adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp soy sauce (optional)
  • A small pinch of salt and pepper (optional)
  • 2 cups cooked Japanese short-grain rice (warm, not hot)
  • 1/4 tsp salt (to season rice or for your hands when shaping)
  • Roasted seaweed (nori) sheets, cut into strips or rectangles
  • Sesame seeds or furikake for garnish
Steps
  1. Prepare the Filling
    • In a bowl, mix the drained tuna with mayonnaise until creamy.
    • Add soy sauce or a pinch of salt and pepper if desired.
    • Set aside.
  2. Prepare Your Work Area
    • Have a small bowl of water and salt nearby. Wet your hands slightly and rub a tiny bit of salt on your palms â€” this helps season the rice and prevents sticking.
  3. Shape the Onigiri
    • Take a handful of warm rice (about 1/2 cup).
    • Flatten it slightly in your hand and make a small indent in the center.
    • Spoon about 1-1.5 tsp of tuna mayo into the indent.
    • Gently mold the rice around the filling to seal it, then shape into a triangle (or round ball if easier).
  4. Wrap With Nori (Optional)
    • Wrap a strip or rectangle of nori around the onigiri. You can place it on the bottom or fully wrap it depending on preference.
  5. Serve or Store
    • Best enjoyed the same day, but they can be refrigerated for up to 1 day.
